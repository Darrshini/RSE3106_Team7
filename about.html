Our Story

T.O.P.H began as a Systems Engineering project aimed at addressing a genuine gap in assistive technology: while tools like white canes and guide dogs help visually impaired pedestrians avoid obstacles, few solutions actively help them judge whether it is safe to cross a road. Early on, the team spoke with a representative from SG Enable / Tech Able to understand the existing landscape — from apps like Seeing AI, Be My Eyes, and OKO, to hardware like haptic belts and UWB-based indoor navigation. That conversation shaped our focus: build something that solves a real, everyday problem rather than something flashy for its own sake, in the spirit of the James Dyson Award. The result is a two-part system: a wearable glasses unit built on a Raspberry Pi 4 with a live camera feed, and a browser-based web app that runs on the user's phone, communicating over a secure WebSocket relay hosted on AWS.
